What is Long Division?

Long division is a fundamental arithmetic algorithm used to divide large numbers (the dividend) by smaller numbers (the divisor). Unlike basic division, which breaks a number into equal parts instantly, long division breaks the problem down into a sequence of simple, repetitive steps.

This calculator is designed not just to give you the answer, but to act as a private tutor. By using the "Next Step" buttons above, you can watch the calculation unfold exactly as you would write it on paper.

How to Do Long Division (The 4 Steps)

The easiest way to remember the long division process is the mnemonic "Dad, Mom, Sister, Brother":

  1. D (Divide): Look at the first digit of the dividend. How many times does the divisor fit into it? Write that number on top.
  2. M (Multiply): Multiply the number you just wrote on top by the divisor. Write the result underneath the dividend digit.
  3. S (Subtract): Subtract that result from the dividend digit to find the difference.
  4. B (Bring Down): Bring down the next digit of the dividend to the right of your subtraction result. Now, repeat the process with this new number!

Example: 175 ÷ 5

Let's use the steps above:

  1. Divide: 5 does not go into 1. So, we look at 17. 5 goes into 17 3 times. Write "3" on top.
  2. Multiply: 3 × 5 = 15. Write 15 under the 17.
  3. Subtract: 17 - 15 = 2.
  4. Bring Down: Bring down the 5 next to the 2. The new number is 25.
  5. Repeat: 5 goes into 25 exactly 5 times. Write "5" on top. The answer is 35.

Why Use the 3 Different Methods?

Math is a universal language, but the notation changes by region. Our tool supports:

  • US/UK (Bracket): The standard method taught in North America and the UK. The divisor is on the left, quotient on top.
  • Europe (Galley/Francophone): Common in France, Spain, and Latin America. The dividend and divisor are separated by a vertical line.
  • Germany (Colon): Common in Germany and Northern Europe. Written as a linear equation (100 : 4 = 25).
